Hi, my name is Ryan Lu and I graduated in May 2020 from Johns Hopkins University with a B.S. Biomedical Engineering.

My interests lie in computational modeling, data science, and neuroscience. I previously worked as a Preclinical Computational Research Intern at Nevro, a medical device company focused on neuromodulation for managing chronic pain. I have also conducted research in several labs within Hopkins.

Additionally, I was the Co-Director of MedHacks, JHU's annual medical hackathon. I served as the Vice President of Service for JHU's service fraternity Alpha Phi Omega and the Director of Finance for Hippocrates Med Review. I was a Head Course Assistant for Intermediate Programming and a Teaching Assistant for Computational Medicine: Cardiology.
